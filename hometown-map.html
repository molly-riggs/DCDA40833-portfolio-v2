Reflection

Map of Danville

Lab six was one of the harder labs for me. I wanted to pick a color design that reflected something meaningful to me, since making a map of my hometown is a meaningful project. For the design inspiration I decided to base it off of my favorite country album by Luke Combs called “This One’s For You”. I had AI give me color codes that translated to the colors of the album, which I used to implement the album’s color scheme into my map. I chose to use the darker colors like grey for the water, and the brighter colors like oranges and reds for the land. When it came to catastrophic challenges, the biggest thing that was important to me was making sure that the map was readable. I like the way that I currently have it set up, where when you zoom in more names of locations and restaurants appear. But while zoomed out, you can only see the pins of places that I had highlighted. If the map was zoomed out and still showed all of the store labels, it would look too cluttered and make it hard for users to read. The current design choice makes it look overall cleaner, and makes sure the map is readable. The only challenge I ran into was when I made updates in my map on the mapbox website, they weren’t showing up in my VS code. I had AI help me figure out how to get these changes that I made online to reflect in my VS code app. This was really helpful in figuring out the problem, since I was constantly making little updates to the map to help better match the color scheme of the album. Overall, there weren’t many issues.
